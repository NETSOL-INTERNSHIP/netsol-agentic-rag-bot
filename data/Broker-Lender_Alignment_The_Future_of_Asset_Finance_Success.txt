Asset finance
is often viewed through a lender-led lens, but our latest report,
“Why digital transformation in asset finance needs an AI gear shift”
, shows that brokers play a central role in how quickly, clearly, and confidently a finance journey moves forward.
Based on proprietary research with 100 asset finance industry leaders, the report highlights a clear point that lender decision-making is partly shaped by the pace, accuracy, and completeness of broker and dealer processes.
When information is complete, accurate, and easy to process, the journey moves faster. When it is delayed, fragmented, or incomplete, friction spreads across the entire chain.
For
NACFB
brokers, this creates a clear opportunity.
Broker-lender alignment
is no longer just about good relationships. It is becoming a competitive advantage.
Why alignment matters more than ever
Customers now expect speed, transparency, and regular updates. They do not always see the complexity behind the scenes. They simply want to know:
Has my application moved forward?
Is anything missing?
When will I get a decision?
What happens next?
When brokers and lenders are not digitally aligned, the customer experience suffers. Brokers end up chasing updates, lenders spend more time checking incomplete information, and customers are left waiting.
Many of the delays customers experience can be traced back to familiar friction points across the journey.
Where friction enters the journey
Some of the biggest friction points in asset finance include slow decisioning, a lack of visibility, complaint handling, and document collection. For brokers specifically, onboarding and payout were also highlighted as acute issues.
This friction often comes from familiar issues:
Missing or inconsistent customer information
Manual document collection
Rekeying data across systems
Limited status visibility
Slow communication between the broker and the lender
Unclear next steps for the customer
None of these problems is new. But in a faster, more digital market, their impact is becoming harder to ignore.
First-time accuracy is becoming a broker advantage
For
brokers
, first-time accuracy can be a powerful differentiator. A complete and well-structured submission helps lenders assess cases faster and reduces the need for repeated back-and-forth.
This does not mean putting the burden only on brokers. Lenders also need to provide the right digital infrastructure, including clearer portals, APIs, better tracking, smoother document exchange, and more consistent communication.
The role of connected technology
The future of broker-lender alignment will depend on more connected systems and processes. APIs, digital workflows, shared data standards, and automation can help reduce duplication and create a clearer view of each deal.
For brokers, this means less time spent on admin and more time spent advising clients. For lenders, it means better-quality submissions and faster processing. For customers, it means a smoother and more transparent finance experience.
The future is collaborative
The next phase of asset finance will not be defined by technology alone. It will be defined by how well brokers and lenders use technology to work together.
The strongest partnerships will combine broker expertise with lender capability and digital infrastructure. Brokers will continue to bring relationship value, market knowledge, and customer understanding. Lenders will continue to bring capital, risk appetite, and underwriting expertise.
But the bridge between them needs to become faster, cleaner, and more connected. That is where the future of asset finance will be won.
